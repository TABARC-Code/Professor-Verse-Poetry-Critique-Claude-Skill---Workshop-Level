# Professor Verse — Poetry Critique at Workshop Level

This is a Claude skill. Ok note, It's not flattering!. so It diagnoses what works in a poem, what doesn't, and why.

The voice is Professor Verse — exacting, precise, grounded in craft. The method is workshop discipline: evidence before verdict, revision priorities ordered by impact, no generic praise. You submit a poem, you get back a diagnosis you can actually use.

---

## What This Skill Does

Critiques poems at beginner, high school, undergraduate, and MFA level. Handles form analysis — sonnets, villanelles, sestinas, haiku, ghazals, pantoums, sapphics, odes. Scansion and prosody. Revision strategy. Manuscript ordering. Spoken word and performance poetry. Prose poetry and lyric essay. Poetry teaching. Study materials. Code tools for poets. And hybrid work, poetry with code.

More specifically:

**Critique modes.** Feedback (what's working, what's failing), Brutal Professor (direct and severe), Line Editor (syntax and lineation), Formalist (metre and form satisfaction), Contemporary Poetry Editor (publishability), Revision Architect (how to rebuild a poem), Teaching (explanation with examples), Diagnostic (when you know something's wrong but can't name it).

**Form and tradition.** 15+ forms taught from first principles. Free verse, formalism, Language poetry, spoken word, slam, experimental work, confessional, workshop standard. Each tradition gets its own diagnostic lens; free verse standards don't apply to a ghazal.

**Practical outputs.** Revision plans, manuscript audits, grade-and-comment feedback, study materials, teaching packets, annotation templates, quiz design.

**Code and automation.** Manuscript analysis (word frequency, abstraction detection, end-word repetition). Draft comparison. Submission trackers. Rhyme helpers. Hybrid poetry-code tools.

**Exam support.** AP poetry (thesis, evidence, commentary). UK exam (GCSE, A-level, unseen poetry, PEE paragraphs, AQA/OCR conventions). Both systems covered separately, so no mixing.

**POETRYcommand codes.** Fifteen shortcuts for common tasks: POETRY-01 (Full Critique), POETRY-02 (Diagnostic), POETRY-03 (Theme and Imagery), POETRY-05 (Device Analysis), POETRY-07 (Image-to-Poem), POETRY-08 (Assignment Help), POETRY-09 (Exam Response), and more. Type a code, get routed to the right workflow.

## Why Use This

Most writing tools produce generic feedback. "More imagery." "Strengthen your ending." "Show, don't tell." These are true, but they're not diagnosis. They're true for everything.

Professor Verse doesn't work that way. It reads your poem against the tradition it's in. It looks at the craft decisions you've actually made, not the craft decisions it thinks you should have made. If you've written a confessional poem in workshop mode, it reads it as a confessional poem — different standards apply. If you're experimenting with Language poetry techniques, it doesn't dock you for breaking grammar rules that Language poetry deliberately breaks.

And it's precise. Not "your ending is weak" but "the final image claims a significance the poem hasn't earned — the emotional weight doesn't match the physical specificity." With line numbers. With what to do about it.

The feedback is delivered in workshop mode, so its respectful but hard (little harsh,). It assumes you can handle honest critique and want to get better. If you want cheerleading, find another tool.

## Getting Started

**Your first critique:**

```
Critique this poem. Tell me what's working, what's failing, and why.

[paste poem]
```

**Line edit:**

```
Give me a line-by-line workshop with specific edits and reasoning.
```

**Using a command code:**

```
Use POETRY-03 and POETRY-05: Analyse the theme, imagery, and what each device does.
```

**UK exam context:**

```
I'm doing an A-level unseen poem. Walk me through how to read this and draft a response.
```

**Revision:**

```
Revise this poem three ways — conservative, moderate, radical. Explain the tradeoffs.
```

**Manuscript work:**

```
Help me order these poems and identify which ones are ready.
```

## What This Skill Is Not

So understand its not a flattering mascot. Not some motivational coach. Not a writing community. Not a marketing tool. Not a rhyme dictionary (though it can explain rhyme strategy to be fair).

You'll get diagnostic feedback, craft reasoning, and revision priorities. You won't get "this is beautiful" unless the poem is beautiful. You won't get vague advice. You won't get told to rewrite your poem into someone else's voice.

---

## The Underlying Philosophy

A poem is a set of craft decisions. Some are working. Some are failing. The failure might be at the level of word choice, lineation, image system, syntax, tone, form satisfaction, or structural logic. Your job is to identify which, diagnose why, and decide whether to fix it or double down.

Professor Verse is here to do the first two.

The third — whether to change your poem — is always your call. If the feedback doesn't land, ignore it. If it lands and you disagree, you're allowed to disagree. The skill's job is precision, not authority.
